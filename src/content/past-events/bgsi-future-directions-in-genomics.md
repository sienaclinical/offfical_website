description:
  - "The Indonesian Ministry of Health recognizes the vast potential in integrating genomics with other omics fields such as proteomics and metabolomics to provide a comprehensive understanding of diseases and advance personalized health management. As part of this vision, the Biomedical and Genomic Science Initiatives (BGSi), launched in 2022, aims to recruit 100,000 participants by 2029, contributing to Indonesia's growing genomic agenda. BGSi has invested in data systems to facilitate data sharing for collaborative research, with plans to build a facility capable of sequencing and storing specimens for 400,000 participants. In 2023, the Ministry introduced significant reforms to enhance clinical research, establishing the Indonesia Clinical Research Center (INA-CRC ) under the MOH's BB Binomika, which serves as the national hub for supporting researchers and managing the clinical research registry. Significant progress has already been made through the BGSi initiative, including the establishment of a biobank network, a high-throughput sequencing facility, and Indonesia's genomics database, paving the way for increased collaboration among stakeholders."
  - "On September 12, 2024, under the theme \"Future Directions in Genomics\" BGSi marked its two-years milestone by hosting an inspiring event aimed at (i) recognizing the progress made so far, (ii) learning from the experiences of other countries in implementing their genomics programs, and (iii) encouraging active participation from a wide range of stakeholders, including researchers and industry leaders."
